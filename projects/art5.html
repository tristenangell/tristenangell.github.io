Giorgio De Chirico

The Enigma of an Autumn Afternoon

This was the first Metaphysical art piece, showing the beginnings of a style that Chirico would later build upon.

Back to Overview

This is the first painting in Chirico's Metaphysical Town Square series. This was the first use of his now famous style and imagery, consisting of old towns lit from long angles with sharp contrasts, evoking a disconcerting, almost dreamlike feeling. The buildings in the piece are simplified, drawing more attention to the lighting of the statue and the couple in the foreground. This piece stands out as the first to depart from Chirico's previous art style and solidify the characteristics of Metaphysical Art.
