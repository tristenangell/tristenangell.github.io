Andy Warhol

Campbell's Soup Cans

One of Warhol's first works, this iconic collection of screen printed soup cans invited in a new way to look at everyday objects

Back to Overview

Warhol had been experimenting with different screen printing techniques prior to creating this piece. He used these techniques to produce a uniform series of Campbell's soup cans, each nearly identical. While others were beginning to create Pop Art at the time, this is widely regarded as one of the pieces that jump started the movement. Warhol followed in the footsteps of Duchamp in that he put less emphasis on the techniques used to create the art and the skill of the artist than the idea behind the art. He was a pioneer of screenprinting techniques in the pop art scene, and this piece stands out in particular as one of the first to focus on an everyday item in modern life.
