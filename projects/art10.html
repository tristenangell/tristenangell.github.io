The Stonebreakers

Gustave Courbet

One of the earliest works of the Realism movement, this artwork eschewed the ideals of romanticism

Back to Overview

Through a simple depiction of a memory, Courbet managed to begin an entire art movement. He painted the scene as it was, including the laborers' torn clothes and the muddy backdrop of the hill behind. He put an equal amount of detail into the rocks and the dirt as he did the people. The colors are natural, depicting things as closely as possible to how they were. This was a conscious rejection of the highly polished art style of his time. His refusal to follow the trends at the time and his insistance on painting things as they truly were kick started the Realism movement.
