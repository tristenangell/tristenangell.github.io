Kazimir Malevich

Black Square

One of the first examples of minimalism in art, and an early example of modern art

Back to Overview

This painting denotes a break from Marc's traditional style of colorful animals against pastoral landscapes, instead featuring brightly colored shapes, full of motion and feeling. Unlike his prior work, there are no clearly visible animals, just the two swirling shapes at war with each other. This was Franz Marc's final work before he enlisted during World War I, later dying in combat. This is one of the earliest works of abstract art, created during the Expressionist movement almost 30 years before Jackson Pollock, Mark Rothko, and others pioneered the Abstract Expressionist movement. It stands on its own as a departure from the artist's previous work and an early example of the idealogies that would take over the art world in the coming decades.
