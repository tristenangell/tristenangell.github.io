Henri Matisse

Woman with a Hat

This painting marked a departure from Matisse's earlier style and kicked off the Fauvism movement

Back to Overview

This was not Matisse's first painting to use the vivid, unnatural colors characteristic of Fauvism, but it was his first real departure from pointillism, and the piece that is considered to have truly began Fauvism movement. Matisse drew influence from the Impressionist art movement that was also going on around that time and some similarities can be drawn with his loose style of brushwork. What truly made this piece stand out however, was a combination of all of those factors. It was a shift in Matisse's use of color, his brushwork, and his use of perspective, all at once.
