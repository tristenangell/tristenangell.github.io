Claude Monet

Impression, Sunrise

The namesake piece for the Impressionist art movement, this painting was the first of several in Monet's characteristic style

Back to Overview

This was Monet's first piece to focus almost entirely on light and color. It was painted as part of a series of six paintings featuring the Port of Le Havre. This particular painting stood out due to its loose brushwork and muted colors, which were unlike Monet's prior work. Perhaps most notably, it was designed to just give the feeling of looking at the Port of Le Havre, rather than showing it in detail. Many other paintings followed with this in mind, but Impression, Sunrise was the one piece that broke away from the ideas of the time and ended up sparking a movement.
